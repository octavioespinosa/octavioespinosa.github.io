About

I am a biostatistician/bioinformatician with a general passion for STEM and science communication.

My work is mostly in the fields of biostatistics, genetics, bioinformatics, biotechnology and machine learning. My PhD focused on discovering relationships between the genome and the phenome in the laboratory mouse using statistical and computational methods. During my post-docs I focused mostly on cancer bioinformatics and cancer drug discovery.

Academic background

  • University of Oxford, UK - DPhil (PhD) (Awarded MRC studentship)
  • Imperial College, London, UK - MSc Bioinformatics (Awarded BBSRC studentship)
  • University of Kent, UK - BSc (Hons) Biochemistry with Medical Biosciences (Class I)
  • Collège du Léman, Switzerland - GCE A-level (UCLES): Mathematics, Chemistry, Biology, French
